Getting Started with kAIzen Coach

1

Connect Your Strava Account

From the home page, click "Login with Strava".

You will be taken to a Strava authorization page. Grant the requested permissions to allow kAIzen Coach to view your profile and activity data.

2

Set Your Goal

After logging in, you'll be directed to the onboarding page.

Fill out the form with your primary training goal, how many sessions you can realistically do per week, and any other lifestyle context that might affect your training.

Click "Analyze My Strava & Build My Plan". The AI will now analyze your data and generate your personalized plan. This may take a minute or two.

3

View Your Plan

Your new training plan will be displayed on the screen. It is broken down week-by-week.

Each session is marked as [KEY], [IMPORTANT], or [STRETCH] to help you prioritize.

You can access your plan at any time by clicking the "Plan" link from the navigation.

4

Get Feedback

After you complete and upload a workout to Strava, you can get feedback on it.

Give the activity a title that reflects the session from your plan that you completed. Add some comments in the private notes (see below for some ideas of what to include) save the updated activity and give the coach a minute or two to analyse it.

Then navigate to the "Get Latest Feedback" page. The coach will analyze your most recent activity against the goals of your plan and provide a detailed breakdown and an execution score.

The coach may occasionally suggest updates to your plan based on your performance, which will be applied automatically.

5

Review Your History

All feedback is saved in your "Coaching Log". You can visit this page to see a complete history of your AI-coached sessions.

Consistent tracking = better injury management

The coach tracks pain levels and progress over time. This helps identify patterns and adjust training to prevent aggravation.

Example progression:

Week 1: "Knee pain 6/10 after intervals"

Week 2: "Knee pain 4/10 on easy runs"

Week 3: "Knee feels fine, pain 1/10"

Important: The coach can suggest reduced load, different exercises, or when to consult a professional based on your notes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my data safe?

Yes. We use the secure Strava OAuth2 protocol to connect to your account. Your training data is stored securely in our database and is only used to generate your plans and feedback.

What do the session priorities mean?

[KEY]:

These are the most important workouts of the week, typically high-intensity sessions that drive the biggest fitness gains. Prioritize these above all others.

[IMPORTANT]:

These are foundational workouts, like your weekly long run or primary strength session. They are crucial for building endurance and resilience.

[STRETCH]:

These are supplementary sessions, like extra recovery runs or mobility work. They are beneficial if you have the time and energy, but are the first to drop if your schedule is tight.

The plan doesn't have workouts assigned to specific days. Why?

kAIzen Coach provides a weekly list of sessions to give you the flexibility to schedule them around your life. As a general rule, you should allow at least one easy or rest day between [KEY] sessions.

What if I miss a workout?

Don't worry! Consistency over perfection is the goal. Simply move on with the plan. If you consistently miss [KEY] sessions, the coach will notice this trend in the feedback loop and may adjust future weeks to be more manageable.

Can I change my goal?

Yes. From the home page, click "Generate a New Plan". This will take you back to the onboarding form where you can input a new goal. The coach will summarize your just-completed training cycle and use it as context to build your new plan.
